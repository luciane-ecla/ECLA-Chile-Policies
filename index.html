Chile · two insurance problems and one capital problem

Opening synthesis. Entity-level, policy-level and site-level detail follows in the subsequent slides.

The three Chilean entities — Frigopacífico, Hook and Multifrigo — operate under distinct balance sheets, banking covenants and insurance programs. Each carries an independent, non-fungible Absorption Capacity (abbreviated RBC — Risk-Bearing Capacity, distinct from the regulatory "Risk-Based Capital"). The portfolio's retention profile breaks down into three tensions.

Insurance problem #1 — Hook (scope gap). The Mapfre policy covers fire and allied perils only. Earthquake is not covered. In a country where seismic risk is the dominant peril, the worst unit (Puerto Varas, TIV ≈ US$ 40M) is fully exposed to the earthquake scenario. Effective retained exposure (ERE) US$ 22.6M – US$ 43.8M (P10–P90), against absorption capacity of US$ 1.3–1.8M.

Insurance problem #2 — Frigopacífico (aggregation). Four sites concentrated in the Biobío region share a single loss limit (aggregate limit UF 1.75M ≈ US$ 68.8M). Under a concurrent event hitting FP3 + FP4, combined gross PML of US$ 49M – 79M approaches or pierces the aggregate limit. Effective retained exposure (net of recovery) ranges from US$ 3.7M – US$ 19.4M (P10–P90), driven by deductibles plus the tail where the limit is breached.

Capital problem. All three entities operate with Cash ÷ Absorption Capacity below 0.25×. Balance-sheet retention is funded via credit lines or policy reimbursement — not through cash on hand.

Methodology — Absorption Capacity

Method, assumptions and haircuts. Single basis applied to all three Chilean entities. Ranges shown throughout this panel are illustrative uncertainty bands, not outputs of a calibrated stochastic model.

Definition

Absorption Capacity — throughout this panel abbreviated as RBC, from Risk-Bearing Capacity (not to be confused with the regulatory "Risk-Based Capital" used in insurance supervision) — is an economic measure of the balance-sheet capital the entity can absorb, without breaching covenants or compromising operational continuity, if a loss lands directly on the balance sheet.

A prudential proxy of 15% of Adjusted Equity is adopted for this panel to enable comparability across entities — neither a regulatory determination nor a contractual limit. This is a working proxy for internal discussion, with prudential estimated adjustments where note disclosure is not broken down in the audited FS — flagged items require confirmation with local controller.
